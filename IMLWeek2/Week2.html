(3 points) Recall that the p-dimensional multivariate Gaussian distribution is defined by a mean vector and a covariance matrix . If X is normal distributed with parameters and , then contains the covariance of each pair of components of X, i.e., \[ Cov(Xr, Xs) = E[(Xr _r)(X_s _s)] = _{rs} = _{sr} \] for all \(1 r, s p\). The diagonal terms \(_{rr}\) are called variances. Recall further that the correlation coefficient is defined as \[ Cor(X_r, X_s) = \frac{Cov(X_r, X_s)}{\sqrt{Cov(X_r, X_r) Cov(X_s, X_s)}} \]

Consider the bivariate case p = 2, and let both variables have mean zero, = (0, 0). Let the variance of X1 be 2.0 and the variance of X2 be 3.0. Given these constraints, find such that Cor(X1, X2) = 0.75.

\[ Cov(X_1, X_1) = 2.0\\ Cov(X_2, X_2) = 3.0\\ Cor(X_1, X_2) = \frac{Cov(X_1, X_2)}{\sqrt{Cov(X_1, X_1) Cov(X_2, X_2)}}=\frac{Cov(X_1,X_2)}{\sqrt{2.0\cdot3.0}}=-0.75\\ <=>\\ Cov(X_1, X_2) = -0.75\sqrt{6}\approx-1.837\\ \] \[ \sum_{X_1X_2}=\begin{bmatrix} Cov(X_1, X_1) & Cov(X_1, X_2)\\ Cov(X_2, X_1) & Cov(X_2, X_2)\\ \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} 2.0 & -0.75\sqrt{6}\\ -0.75\sqrt{6} & 3.0\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

LinDist <- function(point, density1, density2,grid)
{
  px = which.min(abs(point[1] - grid)) 
  py = which.min(abs(point[2] - grid))
    
  f1 <- density1[px,py]
  f2 <- density2[px,py]
  return (0.5*f1/(0.5*f1 + 0.5*f2))
}
